About Me

I am a third-year MSci Mathematics student at Imperial College London. My research focuses on applied algebraic geometry, algebraic statistics, and computational applications. I am passionate about connecting deep theoretical mathematics with practical computational tools, as well as exploring their applications in biology and machine learning.

Research Experience

  • GameTheory Package Development (Nov 2024 – Present)
    Supervisor: Dr. Irem Portakal, Max Planck Institute
    Developing the GameTheory package in Macaulay2, focusing on computing totally mixed Nash equilibria using payoff tensors. Contributed to the Macaulay2 in the Sciences workshop.
  • Applied Dynamical Systems and Computation (Sep – Nov 2024)
    Supervisor: Dr. Boumediene Hamzi, Imperial College London
    Studied kernelisation techniques for KAN and generative AI models. Worked on making models robust in high-dimensional scenarios using kernel flows.
  • Elliptic Curve Cryptography (Jun – Sep 2024)
    Supervisor: Dr. Soheyla Feyzbakhsh, Imperial College London
    Investigated the arithmetic properties of elliptic curves over finite fields, such as Hasse's theorem, and their cryptographic applications.
    Notes
  • Dynamics on Homogeneous Spaces (May – Jun 2024)
    Supervisor: Dr. Marie-Amelie Lawn, Imperial College London
    Presented proofs from Ratner's Measure Classification Theorem to the Equidistribution Theorem. Visualized Margulis' Theorem quantitatively using Python.
