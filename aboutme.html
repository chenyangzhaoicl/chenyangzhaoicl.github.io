Academic Projects

Undergraduate Research Opportunity Programme (UROP)

Algebraic Geometry and Cryptography (Supervisor: Dr. Soheyla Feyzbakhsh)
June-September 2024, Imperial College London

  • Finished a research programme (with department bursary) on algebraic geometry and cryptography under the supervision of Dr Soheyla Feyzbakhsh.
  • Mainly focused on the study of Elliptic Curve Cryptography, and more specifically on finite fields and the deductions of the related properties. Also studied the related maths part including number theory and algebraic geometry (with the book UAG of Miles Reid) at the same time. Also learnt about the basics of cryptography, how it works, what’s the maths behind the trapdoor function and how algebraic geometry, or more specifically, elliptic curve on finite fields act on it to create a strong trapdoor function that can be used as a stronger cryptography system.
  • A brief hand written notes of my studies are in the following link: Handwritten Notes.

Second Year Group Research Project

Dynamics on Homogeneous Spaces: Ratner's Theorems and Applications
May-June 2024, Imperial College London

  • Learnt about the basic knowledge of Manifolds, Lie Groups, and Homogeneous Spaces. Dived deeper into dynamical systems on homogeneous spaces. Learnt about Ratner’s theorems, including the related Haar measure, ergodicity, divergence theorem, etc.
  • Understood and presented the proof from Ratner's Measure Classification Theorem to Equidistribution Theorem. Applied Homogeneous Dynamics in Number Theory. Proved Margulis’ Theorem and visualised its quantitative version through code. ICL-M2R-Visualisation
  • Wrote Sections 5, 7, and Appendices B and D of the paper. Did several visualisations through Python to visualise the ergodic flow on a torus and to visualise the quantitative version of Margulis’ Theorem. Paper can be found here.
