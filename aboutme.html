Academic Projects

Undergraduate Research Opportunity Programme (UROP)

Algebraic Geometry and Cryptography (Supervisor: Dr. Soheyla Feyzbakhsh)
June-September 2024, Imperial College London

  • Intensive literature reading on Elliptic Curve Cryptography.
  • Studied the related mathematics behind ECC. Presented the proofs on the properties of the constructed elliptic curve group on finite field (discrete).
  • Realised some classic algorithms of ECC including Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm and the verification algorithm, and also the attacking algorithms including the Pollard’s rho algorithm.

Second Year Group Research Project

Dynamics on Homogeneous Spaces: Ratner's Theorems and Applications
May-June 2024, Imperial College London

  • Learnt about the basic knowledge of Manifolds, Lie Groups, and Homogeneous Spaces. Dived deeper into dynamical systems on homogeneous spaces. Learnt about Ratner’s theorems, including the related Haar measure, ergodicity, divergence theorem, etc.
  • Understood and presented the proof from Ratner's Measure Classification Theorem to Equidistribution Theorem. Applied Homogeneous Dynamics in Number Theory. Proved Margulis’ Theorem and visualised its quantitative version through code. ICL-M2R-Visualisation
  • Wrote Sections 5, 7, and Appendices B and D of the paper. Did several visualisations through Python to visualise the ergodic flow on a torus and to visualise the quantitative version of Margulis’ Theorem.

First Year Individual Research Project

Phase Transition in One and Two Dimension Ising Model
May-June 2023, Imperial College London

  • Independently researched the mathematics behind the Ising Model.
  • Performed a poster presentation about:
    • Introducing the basic knowledge about the Ising Model and the related mathematical concepts.
    • Explaining the reason why there is no phase transition in the 1-D Ising Model in a mathematical way.
    • Explaining the reason why there is a phase transition in the 2-D Ising Model in a mathematical way.
